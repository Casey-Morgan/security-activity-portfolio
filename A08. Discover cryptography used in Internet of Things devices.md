# A8. Discover cryptography used in Internet of Things devices

## Description
For this activity, I explored how cryptography is used in Internet of Things (IoT) devices. IoT devices are everyday objects connected to the internet, such as smart home devices, wearable devices, and connected appliances. Because these devices collect, send, and sometimes store data, cryptography is important for protecting their communication and information.

## Selected examples
### 1. Smart cameras
![cloud-storage](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7998ab45-fc62-4ed4-89bb-efb543a228c0)

Smart security cameras often send video data to a mobile app or cloud service. Cryptography is used to protect this communication so that the video feed cannot be easily intercepted or viewed by unauthorised people.

### 2. Smart doorbells
![Doorbell](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b8208b0a-80ec-48cb-8402-9e52d8ec4eb4)

A smart doorbell connects to a home network and usually sends notifications, audio, and video to the owner’s phone. Encryption helps protect this data while it is being transmitted between the device, app, and cloud platform.


### 3. Smart watches and fitness trackers
![Watch](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/20ca13ac-52aa-4190-a51a-d812f4c21411)
Wearable devices such as smart watches and fitness trackers often collect personal data like heart rate, activity levels, and location information. Cryptography helps protect this data when it is stored on the device or synced with a phone app.

### 4. Smart home assistants
Devices such as smart speakers process voice commands and communicate with online services. Cryptography helps protect the communication between the device and the service provider, reducing the risk of eavesdropping or tampering.

### 5. Firmware updates for IoT devices
Many IoT devices receive firmware updates to fix bugs or improve security. Digital signatures can be used to verify that the update came from a trusted source and was not modified before installation.


## Explanation
Cryptography in IoT devices is used to protect confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity. It helps keep private data secure, protects communication between devices and apps, and ensures that updates or commands come from trusted sources.

## Reflection
This activity showed that cryptography is an important part of IoT security. Even though users may not always see it, cryptography helps protect smart devices and the data they handle. It also showed that IoT security depends not only on encryption, but also on proper updates, strong passwords, and safe configuration.
