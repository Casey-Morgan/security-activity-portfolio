### 1. Access Control
Many campus buildings and restricted rooms use access control systems such as student cards, staff cards, or keypad entry. These systems make sure that only authorised people can enter certain areas. It reduces unauthorised access to offices, labs, and restricted facilities.

**Security concept:** Authentication and authorisation  


### 2. Surveillance
Security cameras are commonly installed in hallways, entrances, lifts, car parks, and outdoor areas. Cameras help discourage theft, vandalism, and unsafe behaviour. They also support investigations if an incident happens.

**Security concept:** Monitoring and deterrence  


### 3. Identity Verification
Student IDs and staff IDs are used to verify identity on campus. These cards may also be used for library services, attendance, or building access. They help confirm that a person belongs to the campus community.

**Security concept:** Identification and authentication  


### 4. Physical Barriers
Locked doors, gates, fences, security shutters, and staff-only rooms are examples of physical protection. These controls protect equipment, records, and restricted areas from misuse or theft.

**Security concept:** Physical security and asset protection  


### 5. Warning Signs and Security Notices
Signs such as “CCTV in use”, “Authorised personnel only”, and emergency procedure posters are part of campus security. Signs inform people about rules, risks, and expected behaviour.

**Security concept:** Awareness and policy enforcement  


### 6. Emergency Systems
Fire alarms, emergency exits, evacuation maps, and emergency phones are also security-related measures. These systems help protect people during emergencies and improve response time.

**Security concept:** Safety, incident response, and risk reduction  


### 7. Secure Network Access
Campus Wi-Fi often requires a student login or institutional account. This helps protect the campus network from unauthorised users and improves accountability.

**Security concept:** Network authentication  


## What I Learned

This activity showed that campus security is not only about digital systems. It also includes physical controls, identity checks, monitoring, and emergency planning. I learned that many everyday campus features are based on important security concepts such as access control, authentication, surveillance, and risk management.


## Reflection

Before doing this activity, I mostly thought about cybersecurity as something related to computers and networks. After observing the campus environment, I understood that security is broader and includes both physical and digital protection. Campus security uses layered defence, where different controls work together to protect people, buildings, and information.
