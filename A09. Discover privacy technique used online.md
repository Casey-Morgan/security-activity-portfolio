# A9. Discover privacy technique used online

## Description
For this activity, I identified privacy techniques used online to protect personal information, browsing activity, and user identity. These techniques help reduce unnecessary data exposure and give users more control over what is collected and shared.

## Selected examples

### 1. Browser private browsing mode
![Private Mode](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/3dc6acf6-ca70-42e9-8be9-0f63d9a2b580)

Private browsing mode helps reduce the amount of local browsing data stored on a device, such as history, cookies, and form data after the session ends. It improves privacy on a shared device, although it does not make the user fully anonymous online.

### 2. iCloud Hide My Email
![Hide meail](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/03e33037-96fc-4b3e-bf61-6ac317d73c0e)

iCloud Hide My Email lets a user generate a random email address that forwards messages to their real inbox. This helps protect the user’s real email address when signing up for websites or online services. It reduces direct exposure of personal contact details and can also help reduce spam and tracking.

### 3. Safari fraud website warning
![alert-safari](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/7a93b0d6-4651-4b40-8ef9-b716195a9077)

Safari includes a fraud website warning feature that helps protect users from visiting suspected phishing or fraudulent websites. This improves online privacy and safety by reducing the risk of entering personal information into fake websites.


### 4. Passkeys
![Passkeys@2x](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/d226ddb9-2027-402c-92ec-07fd0240de7e)


Passkeys are a modern sign-in method that can replace traditional passwords. They allow users to log in using biometric authentication, a device PIN, or another secure method linked to their device. This improves privacy and security because users do not need to share or remember a password, and it also reduces the risk of phishing.

### 5. Cookie consent and tracking controls
![Accept only](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c83864ea-9620-42a7-b090-0ac31334a3ab)

Many websites now provide cookie settings and tracking controls. These options let users reject some non-essential cookies and reduce tracking for advertising or analytics purposes.


## Explanation
Privacy techniques used online are designed to limit data exposure, protect communications, and give users more control over their personal information. Some techniques focus on secure communication, while others focus on reducing tracking or preventing unauthorised account access.

## Reflection
This activity showed that online privacy is supported by many small features working together. Some are built into websites and apps, while others depend on user choices, such as turning on two-factor authentication or adjusting tracking settings.
