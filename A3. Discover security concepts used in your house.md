# A3. Discover security concepts used in your house

## What I observed
I looked around my house and identified several security measures used to protect people, property, devices, 
and personal information. These included door locks, window locks, passwords on devices, a home Wi-Fi password, 
and outdoor lighting.

## Security concepts identified

### 1. Physical access control
Door locks and window locks are used to stop unauthorised entry into the house. They are basic but important forms 
of physical security.

**Security concept**: Authentication and authorisation


### 2. Authentication
FaceID, TouchID or passwords on phones, laptops, and tablets help make sure only authorised users can access the device.

**Security concept**: User authentication


### 3. Network security
A password-protected Wi-Fi network helps stop unknown people from connecting to the home network. 
This reduces the risk of misuse or attack.

**Security concept**: Network access control


### 4. Deterrence
Outdoor lighting and visible locks can discourage intruders by making the house look harder to target.

**Security concept**: Deterrence


### 5. Privacy protection
Curtains, blinds, and locked personal devices help protect private information from being seen or accessed by others.

**Security concept**: Privacy and confidentiality

## Reflection
This activity showed that home security is not just about locks on doors. It also includes device security, 
network protection, and privacy measures used in daily life.
