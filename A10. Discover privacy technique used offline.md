# A10. Discover privacy technique used offline

## Description
For this activity, I identified privacy techniques used offline to protect personal information in physical spaces and on local devices. Offline privacy techniques help prevent other people from seeing, accessing, or collecting private information without permission.

## Selected examples

### 1. Hide App function
![hide-app](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/01ae2ede-929c-436a-9500-7af0e53c4a06)

The Hide App function allows a user to hide certain apps from normal view on their device. This helps protect private content by making sensitive apps less visible to other people who may briefly use or look at the device. It adds an extra layer of privacy beyond simply locking the phone.


### 2. Privacy screen protector
![Protector](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/34ffdcdb-24dd-4277-aac2-a7b103e29546)
Source: https://www.amazon.com.au/Privacy-Anti-Spy-Tempered-Protector-Easy-Installation/dp/B07NXSXJ83
A privacy screen protector reduces the viewing angle of a phone or laptop screen. This makes it harder for people next to the user to read what is displayed. It is useful in public places such as libraries, cafes, and public transport.

### 3. Shredding paper documents
![thredding paper](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/4ff793c1-1c45-43c8-a6c9-d812177be630)
Source: https://www.amazon.com.au/JIKIOU-Shredder-Portable-Documents-Stationery/dp/B09LCJHVMQ

Shredding printed documents before disposal helps protect personal information such as names, addresses, account numbers, or other private details. This reduces the risk of information being recovered from the rubbish.

### 4. Erase data after 10 failed passcode attempts
![Erase](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/1612268d-da60-4320-a602-4713b35664be)

Some devices allow users to enable a setting that automatically erases stored data after too many incorrect passcode attempts. This helps protect personal information if the device is lost, stolen, or targeted by repeated guessing attempts. It is a strong offline privacy control because it reduces the chance of private data being accessed through brute-force attempts.

### 5. Hidden or limited lock screen notifications
![Notification](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/293de0f3-c4a7-48bc-8459-e95935aaf772)

Many devices allow users to hide message previews or limit what appears on the lock screen. This helps protect privacy by stopping other people from reading private messages, verification codes, or app notifications without unlocking the device.


## Explanation
Offline privacy techniques protect personal information without relying on the internet or online services. They are important because privacy risks also exist in physical spaces, not only in digital systems. These techniques help reduce visual exposure, physical access, and information leakage.

## Reflection
This activity showed that privacy protection is not only an online issue. Many everyday actions, such as locking devices, storing documents safely, and limiting what others can see, are also important parts of protecting privacy.
