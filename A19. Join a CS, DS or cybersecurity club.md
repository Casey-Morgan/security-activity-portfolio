# A19. Join a CS/DS/cybersecurity club

## Description
For this activity, I joined the UWA AI Club. Although it is focused on artificial intelligence, it is still relevant to computing and digital security because AI is increasingly connected to cybersecurity, privacy, automation, and responsible technology use.

## Club joined
Club name: UWA Artificial Inteligence Club

## What I did
I joined the UWA AI Club community and accessed its Discord server. This allowed me to become part of a student technology community where members share information, discuss topics, and participate in activities related to AI and computing.

## Why this is relevant
Joining a CS, DS, or cybersecurity-related club helps build engagement with the wider tech community. These communities create opportunities to learn from others, hear about events, discuss current topics, and develop a stronger understanding of issues related to computing and security.

The UWA AI Club is relevant because AI and cybersecurity are closely connected in several ways:
- AI is used in threat detection and security automation
- generative AI creates new security risks such as prompt injection and hallucination
- AI systems raise privacy, ethics, and data protection concerns
- AI tools are increasingly used in technical and professional environments

## What I learned
This activity showed that joining a student club is a simple but useful way to become more involved in the broader technology community. It also showed that AI-related communities can still contribute to cybersecurity learning because many current security issues now involve AI systems and AI-enabled tools.
