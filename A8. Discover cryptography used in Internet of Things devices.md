# A8. Discover cryptography used in Internet of Things devices

## Description
For this activity, I identified examples of cryptography used in Internet of Things (IoT) devices. IoT devices include smart home products, wearable devices, sensors, and connected appliances. These devices often collect, store, and transmit data, so cryptography is important for protecting that information.

## Selected examples

### 1. Smart home Wi-Fi devices
Devices such as smart plugs, smart lights, and smart cameras usually connect through a protected Wi-Fi network. They rely on Wi-Fi encryption such as WPA2 or WPA3 to protect communication between the device and the router.

### 2. Secure communication with mobile apps
Many IoT devices are controlled through a mobile app. Communication between the app, the IoT device, and the cloud service is often protected using encryption such as TLS. This helps keep commands and user data private.

### 3. Device authentication
Some IoT devices use cryptographic keys or certificates to prove their identity when connecting to a server or another device. This helps prevent unauthorised devices from joining the system.

### 4. Firmware integrity protection
IoT devices may use digital signatures to verify firmware updates. This helps make sure the update comes from a trusted source and has not been changed by an attacker.

### 5. Encrypted stored data
Some IoT devices store data locally, such as recordings, health information, or usage logs. Encryption can be used to protect this stored data from unauthorised access.


## Explanation
Cryptography in IoT devices helps protect confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity. It secures communication, verifies trusted devices and updates, and protects stored information. This is important because IoT devices are often connected all the time and may handle private data.

## Reflection
This activity showed that IoT devices depend on cryptography in many ways, even though users may not see it directly. It also showed that smart devices can create security risks if they are not updated, configured properly, or protected by strong passwords and secure networks.
