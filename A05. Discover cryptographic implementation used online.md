# A5. Discover cryptographic implementation used online

### 1. HTTPS on websites
![Padlock](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/48b55a49-a23d-4f6e-a755-bae6c3f56eb8)

Many websites use HTTPS to protect data sent between the browser and the web server. HTTPS uses TLS, which relies on cryptographic techniques such as encryption, certificates, and key exchange. This helps keep login details, payment data, and personal information secure while they travel over the internet.

Example:
- https://www.google.com
- https://www.github.com
- https://www.bankwest.com.au

### 2. Password hashing in online accounts
![Password-Hashing](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/270971b1-ccf1-4727-9b9a-f5dadf41a14f)

Online services do not usually store passwords in plain text. Instead, they store a hashed version of the password. When a user logs in, the entered password is hashed again and compared with the stored value. This reduces the risk of password exposure if the database is leaked.

Example:
- social media accounts
- email services
- shopping websites

### 3. Digital certificates
![Certificate](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/740c13ad-51aa-4ce3-860c-9db0c2abf827)

Websites use digital certificates to prove their identity. These certificates are issued by trusted certificate authorities. When visiting a secure site, the browser checks the certificate before creating a secure connection.

Example:
- opening the padlock icon in a browser
- viewing certificate details on a secure website

### 4. End-to-end encryption in messaging apps

Some online messaging platforms use end-to-end encryption so that only the sender and intended receiver can read the messages. Even the service provider should not be able to read the message content in normal conditions.

Example:
- WhatsApp
- Signal
- iMessage

### 5. Encrypted online payments
![Encrypted payment](https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/384e8388-e819-4b76-a2e3-1db15646b863)
Image source: https://www.airwallex.com/au/blog/how-encryption-safeguards-payment-data

Online banking and payment websites use encryption to protect financial transactions. This prevents attackers from easily reading or changing payment information during transmission.

Example:
- online banking websites
- PayPal
- Stripe


## Summary
Cryptography used online protects confidentiality, integrity, and authenticity. Encryption keeps information private, hashing protects passwords, and certificates help verify identity. These implementations are essential for secure communication on the internet.

## Reflection
This activity showed that cryptography is used in many online services that people use every day. It is often invisible to users, but it plays a major role in protecting accounts, messages, and financial transactions.
