# A24. Teach your family about cybersecurity topic of your choice

## Description
For this activity, I taught my mum several simple cybersecurity habits through an iMessage conversation. I chose this method because it is natural, easy to understand, and allowed me to explain practical ideas in a family-friendly way.

## Topic taught
The main topic I taught was how to reduce privacy and security risks in everyday messaging and internet use. This included:
- removing tracking parts from shared URLs
- using end-to-end encrypted messaging when possible
- avoiding suspicious links in messages
- not sending passwords in chat
- keeping the phone updated
- using strong login protection on devices

## What I explained
In the conversation, I explained that some shared links contain extra tracking text, often after a question mark, and that this can sometimes be removed to make the link cleaner and reduce tracking. I also explained that end-to-end encrypted channels are safer for private conversations because only the sender and receiver should be able to read the message content.

I also taught that important information such as passwords, bank details, or ID photos should not be sent casually in chat messages. In addition, I explained that suspicious messages should be treated carefully, especially if they create urgency, fear, or pressure to click a link quickly. I also reminded my mum that phone and app updates are important because they fix security problems, not just add new features.

## How I communicated it
I used simple language and short examples so the advice would be easy to understand. I kept the discussion practical and focused on habits that can be applied in daily life, rather than technical terms.


## Reflection
Teaching my mum through iMessage helped me practise explaining cybersecurity in a clear and friendly way. It also showed me that everyday habits, such as checking links and using safer messaging methods, are important parts of personal cybersecurity.
