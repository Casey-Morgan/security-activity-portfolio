## Security concepts identified

### 1. Surveillance
CCTV cameras are used to monitor public areas. They help detect suspicious behaviour, support investigations, 
and discourage theft or vandalism.

**Security concept**: Monitoring and deterrence


### 2. Access control
Some public areas use locked staff-only doors, swipe card needed elevator or ticket barriers to restrict access. 
This helps prevent unauthorised entry.

**Security concept**: Authentication and authorisation


### 3. Deterrence
Visible security guards, warning signs, and cameras act as deterrents. 
They reduce the chance of crime by making people aware that the area is monitored.

**Security concept**: Deterrence


### 4. Asset protection
Anti-theft gates and alarm systems in shops are used to protect goods from theft.

**Security concept**: Loss prevention and detection


### 5. Safety and incident response
Emergency exits, alarm buttons, and help points improve public safety and allow a faster response to incidents.

**Security concept**: Incident response and safety

## Reflection
This activity showed that security in public space is designed to protect both people and property. 
Many controls are visible, and their presence also helps discourage harmful behaviour.
