# A17. Discover 10 different types of locks in use

## Description
For this activity, I identified ten different types of locks used in homes, buildings, vehicles, and personal storage. Locks are one of the most common physical security measures. They help prevent unauthorised access and protect property, equipment, and personal belongings.

## Selected locks

### 1. Deadbolt lock
A deadbolt lock is commonly used on front doors in houses and apartments. It provides stronger protection than a simple spring latch because it cannot be pushed open easily without the correct key or thumb turn.

### 2. Knob lock
A knob lock is built into the door handle and is often used on interior doors. It provides basic security, although it is usually weaker than a deadbolt for external doors.

### 3. Padlock
A padlock is a portable lock often used on gates, lockers, sheds, storage units, and chains. Some padlocks use keys, while others use combination codes.

### 4. Combination lock
A combination lock opens when the correct number sequence is entered. It is commonly used on school lockers, suitcases, bike locks, and small storage containers.

### 5. Cam lock
A cam lock is often used on filing cabinets, mailboxes, drawers, and small cupboards. It is a simple lock designed for light security and convenient access.

### 6. Mortise lock
A mortise lock is installed inside a pocket cut into the door. It is commonly used in commercial buildings and older homes. It is known for being more durable and secure than some simpler lock types.

### 7. Cylinder lock
A cylinder lock uses a rotating cylinder and key to control access. Euro cylinder locks are common in doors in many buildings and can be replaced without changing the whole lock body.

### 8. Smart lock
A smart lock can be opened using a phone app, PIN, fingerprint, or digital credential. It is often used in homes, apartments, and short-stay accommodation.

### 9. Bike cable or U-lock
Bike locks are used to secure bicycles in public or private spaces. Cable locks are lighter and easier to carry, while U-locks are usually stronger and provide better theft resistance.

### 10. Car door lock
Car door locks control access to a vehicle and are usually operated by a physical key, remote key fob, or keyless entry system. They are an important everyday example of access control.


## Explanation
These locks are used in different environments and for different levels of security. Some are designed for strong protection, while others are mainly for convenience or low-risk situations. Together, they show how physical security is applied in everyday life.

## Reflection
This activity showed that locks come in many forms depending on what needs to be protected. It also showed that physical security is still a very important part of security, even in a world with many digital systems.
